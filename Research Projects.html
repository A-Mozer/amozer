Research Projects

Ongoing and completed research

SNPSTR markers for forensic and conservation purposes

Field Projects International & In Situ Labs

Development of SNPSTR markers for lions, bears and elephants for use from trace DNA.

ancient DNA Laboratory

Leibniz Institute for the Analysis of Biodiversity Change, Museum Koenig Bonn (Germany)

The establishment of an aDNA laboratory for the study of the genetic diversity of historical populations.

Golden Eagle Project

SASA Wildlife DNA Forensics Laboratory, Edinburgh (UK)

Forensic validation of STR markers and curation of a DNA database for the Golden Eagle.

Vietnamese Salamander Project

Wildlife Forensic IEBR Laboratory, Hanoi (Viet Nam)

Analysis of mitochondrial loci for species identification of illegally traded Vietnamese salamanders.

Hornbill Project

Clark R. Bavin National Fish and Wildlife Forensic Laboratory, Ashland, Oregon (US)

Analysis of avian sex markers and mitochondrial markers for the identification of hornbills for the development of morphological identification guidelines.

CellGen Project

Leibniz Institute for the Analysis of Biodiversity Change, Museum Koenig Bonn (Germany)

Analysis of mutations in non-transformed cultured cell passages.

FOGS - Forensic Genetics for Species Protection

Leibniz Institute for the Analysis of Biodiversity Change, Museum Koenig Bonn (Germany)

Established SNPSTR markers to combat illegal wildlife trade for over 70 species and demonstrating their applicability through individual identification, relationship testing and geographic assignment.

Red Kite Project

University of Chester (UK)

Development and analysis of molecular markers to monitor genetic variation in reintroduced red kites.

Invasive Species Project

Grenada Forestry Department (Grenada)

Abundance and distribution of invasive cats and mongooses in Grenada.

Rainforest Project

Brownsberg Nature Park (SUR)

Identification, abundance and distribution of various plant and animal species in the rainforest.
